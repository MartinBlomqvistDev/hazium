Hazium began with a simple question.

Could publicly available information have revealed the Swedish fluazinam controversy before it became national news?

Environmental monitoring exists separately from toxicology.

Sales statistics exist separately from exposure pathways.

Residue measurements exist separately from health research.

Each source describes only a fragment of reality.

No single source can explain the whole.

Hazium therefore asks a different question.

Rather than collecting more data,

**can artificial intelligence construct computational representations that make relationships across heterogeneous public evidence discoverable, explainable and investigable?**

The objective is not prediction.

The objective is understanding.

---

# 2. Vision

Hazium seeks to become an explainable machine learning platform capable of constructing continuously evolving computational models of environmental and public health evidence.

The long-term ambition is to help humans investigate emerging hazards before they become widely recognised.

Hazium does not replace scientific expertise.

Hazium augments human reasoning.

---

# 3. Mission

Develop transparent machine learning systems capable of integrating heterogeneous public evidence into computational representations that support investigation, discovery and critical thinking.

---

# 4. Core Beliefs

## Reality is relational.

Environmental and public health hazards do not exist as isolated observations.

They emerge through relationships.

---

## Knowledge is distributed.

No organisation possesses complete knowledge.

Understanding emerges through synthesis.

---

## Models are investigative instruments.

A computational model should not merely describe reality.

It should become an environment within which reality can be investigated.

---

## AI should help humans think.

Hazium should never become a system that replaces human judgement.

Its purpose is to support investigation, not automate conclusions.

---

## Explainability is mandatory.

Any important conclusion should remain understandable.

Opacity is considered a design failure.

---

## Technology follows purpose.

Technologies are introduced only when they improve the system's ability to represent reality or reason about it.

Portfolio value is never sufficient justification.

---

# 5. Non-Goals

Hazium is not:

- a pesticide database
- a dashboard
- a watchlist
- a news aggregator
- a compliance tool
- a replacement for regulatory agencies
- a scientific authority

These may become interfaces.

They are not the product.

---

# 6. Epistemological Foundation

> *"How can computational models become instruments for investigation rather than merely representations of information?"*

Hazium is inspired by investigative methodologies that treat models as environments for reasoning.

The project seeks to construct computational representations within which heterogeneous evidence can be assembled, explored and questioned.

Visualisations are outputs.

The model itself is the product.
